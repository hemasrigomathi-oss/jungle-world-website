WILDLIFE HABITATS

Forest

Forests are dense natural habitats filled with trees and vegetation. They provide food, shelter, and protection for animals such as deer, pandas, panthers, elephants, and many species of birds.

Grass

Grasslands are vast open areas covered mainly with grasses. They are home to grazing animals like giraffes, horses, deer, elephants, zebras, and other wildlife that thrive in open spaces.

Mountains

Mountains have high elevations, rocky terrain, and cooler climates. Animals such as panthers, mountain goats, deer, and other hardy species are specially adapted to survive in these challenging environments.

WetLand

Wetlands are water-rich ecosystems that include marshes, swamps, and riverbanks. They support a wide variety of wildlife, including elephants, deer, elephants, deer, water birds, fish, frogs, and many aquatic species.
