EXTINCT SPECIES

DODO

The Dodo was a large, flightless bird that lived on the island of Mauritius in the Indian Ocean. It became extinct in the late 17th century due to hunting and the introduction of invasive animals by humans. Today, the Dodo is a symbol of extinction and the importance of wildlife conservation.

Passenger Pigeon

The Passenger Pigeon was once one of the most abundant birds in North America. Excessive hunting and habitat destruction hunting and habitat destruction hunting and habitat destruction hunting and habitat destruction led to its extinction in 1914.

Scimitar-horned Oryx

The Scimitar-horned Oryx is a desert antelope known for its long, curved horns. It became extinct in the wild but survives through conservation and reintroduction programs.

Javan Rhinoceros

The Javan Rhinoceros is one of the world's rarest large mammals. Fewer than 100 individuals remain, living in a protected area of Indonesia.Fewer than 100 individuals remain, living in a protected area of Indonesia.

Great Indian Bustard

The Great Indian Bustard is a critically endangered bird found in India's grasslands. Habitat loss and power-line collisions are major threats to its survival.

Bengal Tiger

The Bengal Tiger is a powerful big cat native to the Indian subcontinent. Conservation efforts have helped increase its population, but it remains endangered due to habitat loss and poaching.

Asian Elephant

The Asian Elephant is the largest land mammal in Asia. It plays a vital role in maintaining forest ecosystems but faces threats from habitat loss and human-wildlife conflict.

Snow Leopard

The Snow Leopard is a rare and elusive big cat found in the high mountains of Central and South Asia. It is vulnerable due to habitat loss, climate change, and illegal hunting.
